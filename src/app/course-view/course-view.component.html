Understanding HIV Transmission

HIV is spread through contact with certain body fluids from a person with HIV, including blood, semen, vaginal fluids, rectal fluids, and breast milk.

Key Prevention Methods

  • Consistent condom use: When used correctly, condoms are highly effective at preventing HIV transmission.
  • PrEP (Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis): Daily medication that can reduce the risk of getting HIV from sex by about 99%.
  • Treatment as prevention: People with HIV who take medication as prescribed can't sexually transmit HIV.

Community Perspective

In many rural communities, stigma remains a barrier to HIV prevention. Local clinics now offer confidential counseling and testing services to encourage open discussions about sexual health.
